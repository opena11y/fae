Paid Subscriptions Suspended

The original purpose of the Functional Accessibility Evaluator (FAE) when it was released in 2004 was to raise awareness of web accessibility issues beyond putting ALT attributes on images and labels on form controls, for example use of heading on a web page. FAE went on from there to what I believe was the first website spidering tool to include at least one rule for every WCAG 2.0 Level A and AA requirements, based the best practices of the HTML and ARIA standards. FAE has been a labor of love for many people, but the web and web accessibility has significantly changed since it was first introduced, and this requires a pause to take time to reflect on the future of FAE. There are limited resources to continue the development of FAE to support the latest web standards and the maintenance of a fee-based FAE service is under additional scrutiny from University Auditors.

Actions

  • Monthly meetings will start in April to gather information from current users on their interest and use of the FAE technologies to help in planning the future of FAE. The meetings will be announced using the FAE announcement system.
  • No new paid subscriptions will be accepted.
  • All current subscriptions will be honored, and your current level of service will be extended at no additional cost until the future of the service is determined.
  • FAE is open source, so as always you are free to download and run the service yourself, see the FAE github repository for more information.
  • Please feel free to use the contact form to provide feedback on these changes.
